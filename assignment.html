CEN416

Course Description: This course focuses on implementing and managing IP routing protocols in Cisco networks. Students learn about routing protocols such as OSPF, EIGRP, and BGP, along with network design and troubleshooting techniques.

Course in a paragraph: This hands-on course teaches students how to configure and manage enterprise-level routing solutions using Cisco networking equipment and protocols. Participants learn the fundamentals of IP routing, including static and dynamic routing protocols such as RIP, OSPF, EIGRP, and BGP, and understand how data packets are forwarded across complex networks. The course covers practical skills in router configuration, network troubleshooting, route redistribution, and implementing routing policies. Students gain experience with Cisco IOS commands and develop competencies aligned with Cisco certification paths, preparing them for real-world network administration and design roles.

CEN434

Course Description: This course introduces students to systematic approaches for developing, operating, and maintaining software systems. Topics include software development life cycles, requirements engineering, design patterns, testing methodologies, and project management.

Course in a paragraph: Software engineering is the disciplined application of engineering principles to software development, ensuring that complex systems are built reliably, efficiently, and maintainable. This course covers the entire software development lifecycle from requirements gathering and system design to implementation, testing, and maintenance. Students learn industry-standard methodologies such as Agile and Waterfall, explore design patterns that solve common problems, and understand quality assurance practices including unit testing and integration testing. The course emphasizes collaborative development, version control, documentation, and project management skills essential for building professional software systems.

CIT412

Course Description: This course develops students' entrepreneurial mindset and business acumen. Topics include business planning, market analysis, financial management, innovation strategies, and ethical business practices.

Course in a paragraph: This course equips engineering students with the knowledge and skills needed to transform technical ideas into viable business ventures. Students explore the entrepreneurial process from opportunity identification and feasibility analysis to business model development and resource mobilization. The curriculum covers essential topics such as market research, competitive analysis, financial planning, intellectual property protection, and startup funding strategies. Through case studies and practical exercises, learners develop business plans, understand customer validation techniques, and learn to navigate the challenges of launching and managing technology-based enterprises in today's dynamic economic environment.

EIE411

Course Description: This course examines the internal structure and design of computer systems. Topics include processor design, memory hierarchy, I/O systems, instruction set architecture, and performance evaluation.

Course in a paragraph: This course provides a comprehensive understanding of how computers are designed and organized at the hardware level, bridging the gap between software and physical components. Students study CPU architecture including ALU design, control units, registers, and pipelining techniques that enhance processing speed. The course covers memory hierarchy from cache to main memory to storage, I/O interface mechanisms, and bus architectures that enable communication between components. Learners explore instruction set architectures (ISA), addressing modes, and performance metrics, gaining insights into how design choices affect system speed, power consumption, and cost. This knowledge is fundamental for careers in computer engineering, embedded systems, and hardware design.

EIE412

Course Description: This course introduces the analysis and design of automatic control systems. Topics include mathematical modeling, transfer functions, stability analysis, frequency response, and control system design using classical and modern techniques.

Course in a paragraph: Control systems engineering focuses on designing systems that automatically regulate the behavior of dynamic processes without human intervention, from thermostats to aircraft autopilots. This course teaches students to model physical systems using differential equations and transfer functions, analyze system stability and response characteristics, and design controllers that achieve desired performance. Students explore both time-domain and frequency-domain analysis techniques, including root locus and Bode plots, and learn to implement PID controllers and state-space methods. Practical applications span industrial automation, robotics, power systems, and automotive engineering, making this knowledge essential for modern engineering practice.

EIE413

Course Description: This practical course provides hands-on experience in electrical and electronics engineering through laboratory experiments and a mini project. Students apply theoretical knowledge to real-world problems and develop technical skills.

Course in a paragraph: This experiential learning course allows students to apply theoretical concepts from their electrical and electronics courses to practical implementations in a controlled laboratory environment. Through guided experiments, students work with various instruments, circuits, and systems, developing proficiency in measurement techniques, troubleshooting, and data analysis. The mini project component challenges students to design, build, and test a functioning system that solves a specific engineering problem, fostering creativity, project management skills, and technical documentation abilities. This hands-on experience bridges the gap between classroom learning and professional practice, preparing students for industrial work and advanced research.

EIE416

Course Description: This course covers the principles and applications of electrical measurements and instrumentation systems. Topics include measurement techniques, transducers, signal conditioning, data acquisition, and calibration procedures.

Course in a paragraph: This course teaches students how to accurately measure electrical quantities and convert physical phenomena into electrical signals for analysis and control. Students learn about various measurement instruments including multimeters, oscilloscopes, and spectrum analyzers, understanding their operating principles, accuracy, precision, and error sources. The course covers transducers and sensors that convert temperature, pressure, flow, and other physical parameters into electrical signals, along with signal conditioning circuits that amplify, filter, and process these signals. Students also explore modern data acquisition systems, calibration techniques, and standards that ensure measurement reliability. These skills are crucial for quality control, research and development, and system monitoring in all engineering disciplines.

EIE418

Course Description: This course explores the principles of data communication and computer networking. Topics include network protocols, OSI and TCP/IP models, network security, wireless communication, and network design and management.

Course in a paragraph: This course provides a comprehensive foundation in how data is transmitted and networks are structured to enable global communication. Students study the OSI and TCP/IP reference models, learning how data is encapsulated, transmitted, and received across different network layers. The curriculum covers physical transmission media, data link protocols, IP addressing and subnetting, routing and switching concepts, and transport layer protocols like TCP and UDP. Students also explore network security fundamentals including encryption, firewalls, and authentication, as well as emerging technologies such as wireless networks and IoT communications. This knowledge is essential for designing, implementing, and managing the networked systems that underpin modern computing infrastructure.

GEC410

Course Description: This course covers statistical methods and their applications in engineering. Topics include probability theory, statistical distributions, hypothesis testing, regression analysis, design of experiments, and quality control.

Course in a paragraph: Engineering statistics equips students with mathematical tools to analyze data, make informed decisions under uncertainty, and optimize engineering processes. The course covers probability theory, random variables, and statistical distributions that model real-world phenomena, along with inferential techniques such as hypothesis testing and confidence intervals for drawing conclusions from experimental data. Students learn regression analysis for modeling relationships between variables, design of experiments for efficient data collection, and statistical quality control methods including control charts and Six Sigma principles. These analytical skills are indispensable for research, product development, quality assurance, and reliability engineering across all engineering disciplines.
